About Me

I'm a second-year PhD candidate in neuroscience at UCSF focused on developing computational models of the brain. My current research aims to understand how visual information is processed in the cortex through the lens of transformer-based models. I previously conducted research on auditory circuits and age-related hearing loss in mice, which shaped my interest in sensory computation. When I'm not in the lab, I'm either swimming, running, hiking, biking, or doing some other type of exercise outside.
