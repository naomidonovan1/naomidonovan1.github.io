About Me

I'm a second-year PhD candidate in neuroscience at UCSF focused on developing computational models of the brain. My current research aims to understand how visual information is processed in the cortex through the lens of transformer-based models. I previously conducted research on auditory circuits and age-related hearing loss in mice, which shaped my interest in sensory computation. When I'm not in the lab, I'm either swimming, running, hiking, biking, or doing some other type of exercise outside.

Projects & Research

Functional characterization of mouse primary visual cortex (V1)

Used the V1DeepDive Dataset to functionally characterize the response properties of excitatory neurons in mouse V1

Limited hearing loss in common transgenic mouse lines

Used two-photon Ca2+ imaging to quantify age-related hearing loss in common transgenic mouse models. Demonstrated that while mice on a C57BL/6 background experience progressive hearing loss, B6 mice crossed with B6.CAST-Cdh23 Ahl+ mice have preserved hearing into adulthood.
