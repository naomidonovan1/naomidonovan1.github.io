Cracking the function of layers in the sensory cortex

cortical-layers optogenetics mouse

Hillel Adesnik, Alexander Naka (2018 Dec)

Perspective paper that describes how we can uncover the role of cortical layers in sensory computation and perception. Discuss new methods to optically interrogate layers, to correlate in-vivo function with transcriptional cell type and to map local and long range activity with synaptic resolution.

Your brain on ChatGPT: Accumulation of cognitive debt when using an AI assistant for essay writing task

functional-connectivity eeg human

Nataliya Kosmyna, Eugene Hauptmann, Ye Tong Yuan, Jessica Situ, Xian-Hao Liao, Ashly Vivian Beresnitzky, Iris Braunstein, Pattie Maes (2025 Jun)

Using EEG, explored brain connectivity between participants who either used an LLM, a search engine, or brain only during an essay writing task. Saw that brain-only particpants had strongest, most distributed networks, while LLM users had the weakest connectivity.

Stimulus type shapes the topology of cellular functional networks in mouse visual cortex

functional-connectivity mouse neuropixels functional-motifs network-modularity

Disheng Tang, Joel Zylberberg, Xiaoxuan Jia, Hannah Choi (2024 JuL)

Analyzed Neuropixels data from the Allen Institute, including simultaneous recordings of stimulus-evoked activity in neurons across 6 different regions of mouse visual cortex. Compared the functional connectivity patterns across different stimulus conditions and saw that the overall frequency of functional motifs were the same, but identities of neurons within motifs changed, and the degree to which functional motifs were within a single brain region increased with increasing stimulus complexity.
